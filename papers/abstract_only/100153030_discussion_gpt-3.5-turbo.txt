The abstract presents a study on the mapping between solutions of the Euclidean O(3) non-linear sigma model and the Pohlmeyer reduced theory, specifically the sinh-Gordon equation. The findings suggest that instantons in the O(3) model do not have a counterpart in the reduced theory, merons correspond to the ground state, and elliptic solutions exhibit a two to one correspondence between the two descriptions.

The concept of equivalent descriptions of sigma models in symmetric spaces in terms of integrable systems, such as the sine-Gordon equation, through Pohlmeyer reduction is intriguing. This study sheds light on the relationship between different types of solutions in these two models, providing insights into their correspondence and differences. Understanding how various solutions in the non-linear sigma model translate to the reduced theory enhances our comprehension of the underlying physics and mathematical structures involved.
